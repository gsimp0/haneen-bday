This is a diary entry from 30/12/2205

I hope we have a lifetime. The lifetime I have left may be approximately 57 years. I met Haneen a few years ago in a club we both volunteered for to keep going. She did social media, and I did not very much. We met again at a social neuroscience conference and talked about death. Months later, we got coffee, and she paid for mine. We talked about mountains and our parents. Then she asked me if I’d like to go to Perth with her. I said yes, of course because I thought she was an incarnation of the divine. We rented a van and drove down the coast from Perth to Busselton, Hamlin Bay, and then Denmark. On our drives, we listened to real survival stories, to music queued, and our own story we came up with about two girls named Emilie Gieselle and Jade. We drank coffee at night, and Haneen did her thing, where she asks a million questions, and we would stay up late talking until my eyes felt dry and she had 12 more in the bank, and I would turn over, and so we would say good night in the way that two friends would. We explored caves and pointed at flocks of small things. We picked our way over elephant rocks and admired coastal plants. I waded into the ice-cold water and felt it deep in my bones. I felt as much of the sea that day as the ray that glided by my legs in the shallows. I fell asleep in our van after Haneen washed the salt water from my hair. When I woke at 3 pm I was incredulous at the time. Time seemed to pass in strange ways on that trip. We ate fancy chocolates and drank coffees from eco cups, which it seemed WA did better than anywhere else. One evening, Haneen and I juggled a soccer ball for rounds. We both took turns juggling for a goal number, 10, 15, 25. In the end, her number was higher than mine, and I enjoyed watching her artfully pass it between her thighs up and down with determined concentration. Back in Perth Haneen attended her friend’s wedding, and that evening I came to the wedding reception with her impromptu. She had called me that afternoon and told me she needed to go, and that I was welcome to go with her. So she picked me up, and we went to Kmart, where I stressfully searched for a dress I hoped I would feel comfortable in and a pair of shoes she chose. Then I sat in her car with her and attempted to do some kind of makeup from what she had I think I just curled my eyelashes and put on some blush that was a wonderful evening to spend with her and I loved sitting with her just the two of us the whole time because she knew the bride and I didn't know anyone except for her. We stayed up very late that night talking later than I thought we would, than I have with someone in a long time. In my head, I saw it as romantic. I loved staying up into the wee hours of the morning just to talk, or to feel her leg brush against mine. The next morning, I readied myself to leave and packed my things for the airport, Haneen didn't need to fly out until later that day, but she said she had an idea so I put my things in the car, and she said it was a surprise. We drove down the street and she turned into a car park next to some big Green Fields there we walked over to a coffee shop. It was the most lovely surprise. She bought us a croissant she said she had one more thing for us to do so we drove towards the airport and then she took a turn off to a smaller car park and we were at a viewing point where you can see the planes take off and land we sat there for a while and watch them and talked about what it would be like to fly them and watched a few of the people around us also witnessing them. Then she dropped me off to the departures section and gave me the biggest hug and I gave her the biggest hug back I turned away with my bags and walked along walk a long over to the big glass doors when I turned around inside the doors I could see her still standing there watching me from a distance and I felt my heart grow so big and I walked inside with the most wonderful feeling that she was going to be someone important.
